Srisailam places Famous main Another of the top unexplored places to visit in Hyderabad, Srisailam is 212 kilometres away from the city and is a famous temple town in the state of Andhra Pradesh. Srisailam is known for its Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga at the main temple and also the Ista Kameshwari Temple. The road going to this temple passes through beautiful dense forests and the whole ambience is just surreal to say the least. The Akkamahadevi Caves go up to 80 feet deep and are another major attraction in this town along with the Srisailam Tiger Reserve where you can view the king of the jungle in all his splendor.
Mallela Theertham Waterfalls The waterfall can be accessed at a distance of 185 kilometres from Hyderabad city and is one of the most picturesque waterfalls in Telangana. The Nallamala forest range surrounds the waterfall, creating a spectacular sight when the water gushes down in full force to the cliffs at a depth of close to 150 feet. The water also crashes on the majestic rock shaped like a Shiva Lingam. This is a hotspot during the monsoons and swimming in the plunge pools near the waterfall is a fun activity for travelers.
Warangal Warangal is one city that will take you back in time through the annals of history. The beautifully built Warangal Fort is a key draw here along with the famous Bhadrakali Temple and huge Ramappa Lake. The distance of the city from Hyderabad is only 145 kilometres and you will require approximately 3 hours to reach here. This charming destination is one of the best unexplored places to visit in Hyderabad. These are some of the hotspots near Hyderabad that you must visit for secluded and relaxing vacations amidst the peace and quiet that you ardently desire. These places offer a glimpse of history, culture and of course, natural beauty that is hard to find elsewhere.
Baobab tree at Hyderabad Golf Course Hyderabad might have earned the reputation as one of the world’s tree cities, but the one tree that has always stayed in the spotlight is the 800-year-old Baobab Tree in Naya Qila. Native to Madagascar, it is said that the wandering fakirs planted a Baobab sapling in the city hundreds of years ago. Standing tall and strong to this day, this massive tree is located within the Hyderabad Golf Course near Golconda. The locals call it ‘Hathiyan ka Jhaad’, referring to its elephantine trunk. Its circumference measures about 25 m and its most interesting feature is its huge hollow that can accommodate up to 40 people. Although visitors are now prohibited from entering the hidden space within the tree, the locals still stop by here for a mere sight of this tree that has existed for ages.
Ananthagiri Hills Ananthagiri Hills are a major hotspot for trekkers looking for solitude away from the hustle and bustle of Hyderabad city. The picturesque hills will delight you along with their intricate trekking trails and natural beauty. There are several waterfalls along the way along with the Nagasamudram Lake and Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple. There is ample scope for trying your hand at adventure activities like rock climbing, balancing boards, Spider web and Tarzan swing among others.
Nelakondapalli This town is a famous Buddhist site, making it one of the top places to visit near Hyderabad. There is a bronze idol of Lord Buddha along with several Maha Stupas, wells, cisterns and Buddhist Viharas. The town is also believed to have found a mention in the Mahabharata and is the birthplace of Bhakta Ramdas the worshipper.
